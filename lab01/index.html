Shirazi Salad (Persian Salad)

A quick an easy Iranian side dish for your main meal

Recipie by Yumna Jawad from feelgoodfoodie

Why This Recipie

Lately I've been trying to eat healthier and wanted to try making salads. I was never really a salad person aside from eating chana chaat (Pakistani appetizer). I wanted an easy salad that still tasted somewhat familure to my current pallete (as someone who primarily eats Paksitani food). So after researching some recipies, I found one that I liked the most and now use often: this recipie. I hope you like it!

Ingredients

  • Cucumbers, ideally Persian cucumbers but regular works fine
  • Tomatoes, ideally firm ones (ex:roma) but any tomatoes work
  • Red Onion
  • Fresh herbs, Cilantro and parsely are commonly used either or both, dried mint is also used pick what you prefer
  • Dressing
    • extra vigin olive oil
    • lime juice
    • salt and pepper
Steps
  1. Dice/chop all of the ingridents (minus the dressing) and make them unifrom
  2. For the dressing, its recommended to whisk all the dressing ingridients togethor in the serving bowl, and then add the salad ingridients and toss for less cleanup
Tips and Tricks
  1. De-seed tomatoes for ideal results: there's a lot of water in tomatoes especially when diced. So if you have the time, remove the seeds and save it for something else.
  2. Chop everything evenly: It helps making your salad look good and make bites more enjoyable.
  3. Toss salad immediately before serving: The salad should be fresh and crunchy, so tossing it right away gives the best results.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning with sald and lime juice: Depeding on size and taste of the salad, you may need to adjust to your liking.
